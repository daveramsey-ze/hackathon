Talking to Someone with PTSD

Talking to someone with PTSD requires sensitivity, patience, and understanding. Here are some key strategies:

  • Listen Actively: Let them share at their own pace. Ask open-ended questions and avoid interrupting. This builds trust and shows genuine interest (PTSD UK, Embark Behavioral Health).
  • Create a Safe Space: Ensure a comfortable, distraction-free environment. Maintain eye contact, offer a hug if appropriate, and reassure them of your presence (Embark Behavioral Health).
  • Avoid "Fixing" Things: PTSD has no quick fixes. Validate their feelings without trying to provide immediate solutions (HealthyPlace, Health).
  • Know Their Triggers: Ask about their triggers and how to help manage reactions. Avoid causing distress (HealthyPlace, Health).
  • Encourage Professional Help: Support them in seeking therapy, but understand it must be at their own pace (Embark Behavioral Health).
